<div class="wp-block-uagb-info-box uagb-block-9fd98309 uagb-infobox__content-wrap  uagb-infobox-icon-above-title uagb-infobox-image-valign-top"><div class="uagb-ifb-content"><div class="uagb-ifb-title-wrap"><p class="uagb-ifb-title-prefix">The Threat Simulation Theory (TST) posits that dreams evolved as a survival mechanism. By simulating dangerous scenarios—being chased, attacked, or social failure—the brain rehearses responses in a safe environment. </p></div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-uagb-info-box uagb-block-ef565816 uagb-infobox__content-wrap  uagb-infobox-icon-above-title uagb-infobox-image-valign-top"><div class="uagb-ifb-content"><div class="uagb-ifb-title-wrap"><p class="uagb-ifb-title-prefix">This strengthens the fight-or-flight reflex for real-world threats. Analyzing nightmares through this evolutionary lens reveals that many &#8220;scary&#8221; dreams are actually adaptive biological exercises designed to sharpen our ancestral survival instincts.</p></div></div></div>
