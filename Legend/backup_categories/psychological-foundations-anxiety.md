<div class="wp-block-uagb-container uagb-block-e22a1dc9">
<div class="wp-block-uagb-advanced-heading uagb-block-c5054e2a"><p class="uagb-heading-text"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urban_legend">Urban legends</a> are not merely scary stories; they are &#8220;anxiety containers&#8221; for society. Psychologically, these tales thrive on the &#8220;Friend of a Friend&#8221; (FOAF) effect, which lends a layer of perceived credibility. They often function as cautionary tales, reflecting collective fears regarding safety, health, or technology. </p></div>



<div class="wp-block-uagb-advanced-heading uagb-block-72f3541d"><p class="uagb-heading-text">By analyzing the psychological triggers—such as the &#8220;disgust reflex&#8221; or &#8220;moral panic&#8221;—we can understand why certain stories go viral while others fade. These legends serve a social function by reinforcing community boundaries and shared values through fear.</p></div>
</div>
